RPG Prototype

Role Playing Game developed in Unreal Engine 5
Repo

Introduction

Prototyped a role playing game level, drawing inspiration from Ghost of Tsushima's combat mechanics.

Concept

The game showcases a high caliber attack combo system. The system encompasses an extensive range of attack types including light attacks, heavy attacks, charge attacks, combo attacks, jump attacks and features mechanics such as directional dodging and target locking. It effectively discourages players from relying on button smashing, fostering a more strategic gameplay experience.

Key Technical System

State Manager

Initially, boolean variables were used to represent states. However, as the number of states grew, maintaining them became a challenge. To address this, enums were introduced as a replacement. However, a new issue emerged with the introduction of enemies, as they had their own distinct states, resulting in a cumbersome system. To tackle this, gameplay tags were implemented for their ease of grouping. States were checked each time an action was performed, resulting in scattered code. To streamline this process, a state manager was introduced. This manager now oversees the process: when the player transitions into a specific state, it triggers the corresponding actions and handles necessary clean-up upon exiting the state. This approach significantly improves code manageability.

Target Locking

Target locking is achieved through a sphere trace. The player's world location serves as the starting point of the trace, while the trace length is determined by adding it to the player's forward vector, which acts as the trace endpoint. If any pawn intersects with this trace, the player's camera orientation is adjusted. To achieve this, the following steps are taken:
  • Turning off "Orient Rotation to Movement" : This prevents the player from automatically facing the direction of movement
  • Enabling " Use Controller Desired Rotation" and setting control rotation : This process is executed by employing the 'Find Look At Rotation' function, where the player's location serves as the origin, and the overlapped enemy from the trace acts as the target. This operation exclusively involves yaw and pitch rotations, guaranteeing that the player consistently maintains focus on the enemy.

Comic Shooter

First Person Shooter developed in Unreal Engine 5
Repo

Introduction

Prototyped a 2.5D First Person Shooter game, incorporating 3D movement mechanics while utilizing 2D characters and environment sprites.

Concept

This game is inspired by DOOM. It's an endless game where you score points by defeating enemies. As you progress, you unlock new weapons by taking down foes. Additionally, the game rewards players with vital resources such as ammunition and health, although the frequency of these pickups diminishes as the game advances, intensifying the challenge.

Key Technical System

Sprite Rotation

Initially, a "look at" rotation method was employed to calculate sprite rotation on each tick. However, a limitation arose when rotating around the character: it caused a change in direction of the sprite when completing a 180-plus degree turn.
To address this limitation, a custom approach was devised. This involves obtaining two vectors: one from the player's location to the enemy's location (vector a), and another from the player's location to the enemy's intended destination (vector b). The direction of the sprite relative to the player is then derived by calculating the dot product of b-a with the player's right vector(vector c).
By leveraging vector math and factoring in the player's position, the system ensures consistent orientation relative to the player's perspective and accuracy in sprite rotation. However, when the character is close to the player in combat, this leads to sprites consistently rotating left and right, which is not the desired result. So, when the character is closer to the player, only the "look at" rotation is used.

Weaponry: Gameplay Dynamics
Two weapon types: one implemented with line trace (fist, pistol, shotgun, machinegun) and the other with projectiles (laser gun, plasma gun, rockets).
For the shotgun line trace, six distinct line traces are executed with a randomized spread. This means that the closer you are to the target, the less spread there will be. This replicates the mechanics of a real-life shotgun, adding an extra layer of authenticity to the gameplay experience.
Additionally, for the machine gun, a small random deflection is introduced. This element of unpredictability heightens realism in the gameplay experience.

Enemies AI: Behavior trees and custom tasks
In the game, there are four types of enemies: ranged, melee, hybrid, and flying. The core behavior shared among them involves navigating to random points within a designated area (nav volume). However, upon sensing the character, their actions vary by type:
  • Ranged Type: Opens fire from a specified distance.
  • Melee Type: Pursues the character and initiates an attack when in close proximity.
  • Hybrid Type: Employs a service that checks the distance to the character every 0.5 seconds. Depending on the distance:
    • If < 700 units, triggers melee attack.
    • If > 700 and < 800 units, triggers ranged attack.
    • Otherwise, chases the player to maintain a distance within 800 units.
  • Flying Type: Pursues the player and, when within range distance, conducts a line trace to determine if it can launch a projectile. If unable to do so, it engages a custom task for dynamic rotation around the player, following the equation:
    • x = player_x_position + r * cos(θ)
    • y = player_y_position + r * sin(θ)
    where θ is the desired angle for the enemy's rotation and 'r' represents the distance between the player and the enemy.

Stuck In a Root

Endless Runner developed in Unreal Engine 5
Repo

Introduction

Prototyped a distinctive endless runner game within a 48-hour time frame (solo dev) for Global Game Jam, aligning with the theme 'roots'. Post competition, the game underwent refinement to elevate overall quality and enhance player experience.

Concept

The game's unique gameplay mechanic involves altering input mapping upon picking up items, with options including suspension or swapping of input for a brief duration.
Pick Up Mechanic
Suspend left input for 2-3 seconds
Suspend right input for 2-3 seconds
Swap right and left key controls for 2-3 seconds

Key Technical System

Procedural Generation

The character maintains a constant forward motion along the x-axis. A pipe with varying heights is dynamically spawned. One of it's component is a triggerable box which is positioned at the end of the static mesh. If the player overlaps it, then a new pipe is appended and the previous pipe is destroyed if it's out of view.
To spawn actors within the pipe, a random point is chosen along the x-axis. Next, a random vector is generated and projected onto the yz plane (perpendicular to the x-axis). Then, a sphere trace is conducted from the random point in the direction of the projected random vector. If the collision only involves the pipe, indicating no presence of other actors, the desired actor is spawned and its orientation is adjusted based on the surface normal obtained from the sphere trace.

There are seven distinct types of blocking enemies in the game, each with the potential to defeat the player: three types of lightsabers, a fence, a ghost, lava, and a skeleton.
While lava and ghosts utilize the previously mentioned spawning system, the remaining enemies simply spawn within a pipe of sufficient height to encompass their width. This design choice ensures that enemies spawn seamlessly, without concern for collisions with one another. This approach is not only crucial for these types of enemies, as they require the full extent of the pipe's space for their proper functioning and behavior, but it's also optimized. Otherwise, we would need to perform a trace throughout the pipe to find an empty section, which can be resource intensive and less efficient.
